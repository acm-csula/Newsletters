For those of you who are unfamiliar with the Association for Computing Machinery, it is an organization that brings together computing educators, researchers, and professionals to promote discussions, share resources, and address various challenges relating to computing. Here at Cal State LA, the ACM Chapter is an ever-growing club where we primarily focus on supplementing the CS program to help guide new and returning students through to their academic goals.

Our slogan is . . .

"IT IS NOT A BUG, IT IS A FEATURE!"

Our Mission

  • Create future leaders by offering leadership opportunities to members who show interest
  • Promote professional growth by providing resources specific to career development
  • Inspire creativity by introducing students with projects that help steer their interest
  • Encourage social involvment by creating events that bring students together through common interests
  • And most of all, guide you to your success!

Projects

This Fall, we are going to have two coding workshops tailored for both lowerclassman and upperclassmen, but either are open for everyone. Just be aware that the upperclassmen workshop will be challenging for those who do not dedicate their time to the workshops.

Lowerclassmen
Game Development

Upperclassmen
Webapp Development


Focus on creating a chat web application similar to Discordapp with the use of React.js, JavaScript, JSX, and Firebase.

The project for the workshop will have you create a Single-Page chat web application that is similar to Discord and Slack. You will learn to Conceptualize and Design a web application that utilizes industry used technology. You will also learn common practices and code management that will help you become a better web and applications developer.
